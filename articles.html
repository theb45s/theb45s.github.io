The B45s at The Phoenix in Fort Wayne, IN on 21-Nov-2014: National Rock Review

The B45’s are a mix of 60’s beat music and rock and roll full of spunk and talent.
Ben Tarr (guitar and vocals), Colin Taylor (bass and vocals), Kellen Baker (lead guitar and vocals), Sam Clay (drums) are the B45s. They sport three-part Beatlesque harmonies and great originals. All are parts of other bands who came together to play a one-time gig at a retirement home. That one gig made them realize they had chemistry. Three-fourths of the band met in a jazz program at the local community schools, and all were participants in the Rock Camp program at Sweetwater Sound. Studies by Day, Jams by Night: Journal Gazette

Teen bands loaded with talent, changing local music landscape
The crowd of a couple thousand who had carried their folding chairs and blankets onto the thick grassy areas and wide sidewalks of the downtown library was restless; even uninterested. The second band of the evening at May’s Beatlefest droned unemotionally through one song after another with little success of providing a fraction of the famed group’s energy. The only commonality it had with the Beatles was it sang the same lyrics. But to cut it some slack, Beatles music wasn’t exactly that group’s specialty. As a result, not long into the band’s 45-minute set, several in the audience milled about or talked as they paid little attention to the uninspiring effort on the plaza stage.
